Submarine cables carry over 99% of international data; the risk is repair. The scarce asset is a ready cable ship, not more fiber. Two-thirds of repair ships reach end-of-life by 2040. Sustaining the fleet needs about $3 billion for roughly 20 ships.

About

What Bottleneck Briefs is

Bottleneck Briefs traces a single industrial bottleneck to its irreducible constraint — the permit, qualified material, queue slot, certification, repair berth, or waste outlet that actually paces capacity — then names the cases and maps who controls it. Each column is built from a research appendix that logs every figure with its source, its date, and a confidence flag: two independent sources, or one primary. The site generator and editorial standard are open source, so the method can be read and reproduced.

View the source on GitHub →

How we source

Every load-bearing number is traced to a primary or near-primary document. High-confidence claims are stated plainly; medium-confidence claims are attributed; single-source or contested figures are flagged, never presented as settled fact. When sources conflict, the range is shown rather than a cherry-picked value.
