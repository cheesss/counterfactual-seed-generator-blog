What Bottleneck Briefs is

Bottleneck Briefs traces a single industrial bottleneck to its irreducible constraint — the permit, qualified material, queue slot, certification, repair berth, or waste outlet that actually paces capacity — then names the cases and maps who controls it.

The method

Each column begins as a deep research note, not an opinion. The note logs every stated figure in an evidence ledger with its source, its date, whether the source is primary or secondary, and a confidence flag. A claim is treated as high-confidence only with two independent sources or one primary source; medium-confidence claims are attributed in the text; single-source or contested figures are flagged and never presented as settled fact. When sources conflict, the range is shown rather than a cherry-picked value. The published column is an edited surfacing of that note — the appendix holds several times the sourced material the column uses.

Two ways to read the archive

Every column is tagged on two axes. The constraint shape (the archetype) is the kind of gate — permits, qualified materials, queue or slot scarcity, validation evidence, repair capacity, or residual handling — and the same shapes recur across unrelated industries. The sector is the industry — energy and grid, defense, healthcare, AI and data centers, water, aviation, telecom. The home page maps how the columns connect by shape, and the archive filters by both.

Corrections & revisions

Columns are revised as evidence improves; a substantively updated column carries an "Updated" date in its byline. Material factual corrections are made in place. If you find an error, the fastest route is an issue on the GitHub repository.
