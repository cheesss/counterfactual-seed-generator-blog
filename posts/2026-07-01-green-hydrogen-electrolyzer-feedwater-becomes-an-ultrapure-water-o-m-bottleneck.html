Central uncertainty preserved: public documents show named projects buying UPW skids, permitting water supply/discharge, and citing conductivity/water-quality targets; they do not yet prove that UPW O&M is a dominant cause of delay or downtime across the sector. The working thesis should therefore remain conditional: feedwater becomes bottleneck-like.

Bottleneck shape selected: validation-evidence.

Where the bottleneck sits

Seed continued mechanically: Green Hydrogen Electrolyzer Feedwater Becomes An Ultrapure-Water O&M Bottleneck.

Evidence trail

  • The “water bottleneck” splits into three different constraints.
  • Bulk water quantity: often headline-grabbing, but usually smaller than agriculture/thermal-power water use at regional scale.
  • Project water sourcing and discharge: locally important where projects rely on reclaimed wastewater, seawater desalination, closed basins, or public sewer/discharge consents.
  • UPW validation/O&M: the argument’s strongest technical bottleneck candidate. The scarce item is a validated, continuously monitored water-treatment and polishing system that keeps impurities below OEM limits and preserves stack life.
  • Project evidence shows water-treatment spend, but not yet universal water-driven delay.
  • Shell Holland Hydrogen I has a named UPW supplier and disclosed demineralized-water need of 700 m3/day.
  • HySynergy I discloses a 20 MW alkaline system requiring 4.5 m3/h of UPW below 5 µS/cm.
  • Kassø discloses a 50 MW PEM system requiring 10 m3/h of UPW below 0.1 µS/cm.
  • Plug Limestone’s EA documents reclaimed-water supply, RO/DI treatment, storage, wastewater pond, and solids.
  • High Marnham shows permitting and water-use documentation as an explicit regulatory gate.

Named cases in the search

  • Shell Holland Hydrogen I: UPW equipment integration and demineralized-water sourcing
  • ACES Delta: Water rights vs UPW O&M vs storage/compressor commissioning
  • Plug Power Limestone Green Hydrogen Project: Reclaimed-water supply, RO/DI treatment, residual handling
  • Plug Power Georgia plant: Operating disconfirmation: water not visibly binding
  • HySynergy I: Grundfos/EUROWATER case pages and PDF; Everfuel inauguration and grid-stabilization releases; Offshore Energy delay article
  • Kassø e-methanol: Grundfos/EUROWATER case pages and PDF; European Energy project update; Reuters opening article; Siemens Energy order reports

What could break the thesis

  • ACES Delta disconfirms bulk-water scarcity as the universal gate.
  • DOE/FONSI evidence says ACES had sufficient groundwater/surface rights and no adverse local/regional water impact under reviewed assumptions.
  • The visible project risk was not water rights but integrated commissioning of 220 MW electrolysis, salt caverns, compressors, and offtake/power systems.

What to watch

  • Whether any operating 20-220 MW green-hydrogen project has documented unplanned downtime directly caused by feedwater quality, resin exhaustion, EDI failure, sensor calibration, TOC/silica/metals contamination, or OEM water-spec noncompliance.
  • Whether UPW system vendors have pricing power, long lead times, limited qualified status, or high recurring service attachment.
  • Whether OEM warranty terms make water-quality records a bankability gate for lenders/insurers.
  • Whether reclaimed wastewater and seawater-derived projects have materially higher O&M cost or permit delay than municipal/industrial-water projects.
  • Whether direct-seawater or lower-purity electrolysis will remain research-stage or become bankable before large project waves reach FID.

Research context

  • Reason for shape: the strongest searched evidence does not yet show bulk freshwater availability as the universal binding constraint for green hydrogen. It more strongly shows a narrower gate: projects must continuously convert variable raw water, reclaimed water, municipal water, or seawater-derived water into electrolyzer-acceptable ultrapure/deionized.
