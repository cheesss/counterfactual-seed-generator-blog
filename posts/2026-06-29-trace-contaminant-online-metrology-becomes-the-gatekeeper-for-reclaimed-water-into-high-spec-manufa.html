Trace-Contaminant Online Metrology Becomes The Gatekeeper For Reclaimed Water Into High-Spec Manufacturing

By Bottleneck Briefs · Published 2026-06-29 · 5 min read

Water reuse is moving from a sustainability story into a measurement story.

The old question was whether a plant could treat municipal or industrial wastewater to a useful quality. The new question is whether the plant can prove, continuously and defensibly, that the treated water is clean enough to enter a process where a few ions, particles, organics, microbes, or fluorinated compounds can affect yield, release, corrosion, or regulatory status.

That shift is clearest in semiconductor fabs. A leading-edge fab does not buy "water" in the ordinary sense. It runs an engineered water system: reclaimed water, reverse osmosis, ion exchange, EDI, polishing, degasification, UV, ultrafiltration, point-of-supply testing, point-of-use testing, and excursions that can divert water out of the process path. The bottleneck is no longer only the membrane or the tank. It is the confidence interval around the measurement.

TSMC's reclaimed-water buildout in Taiwan shows the direction. The company says its Southern Taiwan Science Park reclaimed water plant uses real-time automated quality monitoring and serves as a combined processing, monitoring, and supply center. It began with 10,000 metric tons per day in 2022 and is expected to reach 36,000 metric tons per day in 2026. TSMC also says new Hsinchu fabs will use 100% reclaimed water for 2 nm production and that reclaimed water will replace more than 60% of Taiwan fab water by 2030. Those are not generic conservation claims. They imply a manufacturing system in which reclaimed water must be qualified, monitored, and controlled as an input to advanced production. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Intel's Ocotillo expansion is another signal. A 2024 environmental assessment says the new fabs would use about 14 million gallons per day, including 7.9 MGD of reclaimed water. The document also says external and on-site reclaimed water are mixed to supply UPW and cooling tower systems. That does not disclose Intel's full internal release protocol, but it does show reclaimed water moving into the infrastructure that feeds high-purity manufacturing water. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Samsung Austin gives the cleanest public description of the control logic. Samsung says it used 960 million gallons of recycled water in 2021. A separate fab-water interview says wastewater and UPW rinse streams are monitored at several points and diverted for further treatment or waste treatment if QC parameters are exceeded. That is the practical gate: measured quality determines whether reused water continues toward production or exits the pathway. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

The measurement problem is tightening because the process window is shrinking. SEMI's F121 discussion says IRDS identified 3 nm as a critical particle size and notes that particles can form from dissolved particle precursors. IRDS says UPW particle detection has struggled to keep pace with killer-particle requirements and that 10 nm particle metrology was not yet available as a proven capability in the roadmap text reviewed. That creates a paradox: fabs need more reclaimed water, but the contaminants that matter most are harder to measure. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

Pharmaceutical water systems show a parallel but more regulatory form of the same pattern. EU GMP Annex 1 says WFI systems should include continuous TOC and conductivity monitoring, with sensor locations justified by qualification. It also ties chemical, microbiological, and endotoxin results to return-to-service and batch certification decisions. USP <643> and <645> allow online or offline TOC and conductivity, but USP <643> makes clear that TOC does not replace microbiology or endotoxin control. :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

That distinction matters. Conductivity and TOC can be online release gates. Silica, boron, particles, dissolved oxygen, ozone, and other UPW parameters can be monitored online or near-online depending on the system. Rapid microbial tools exist. But PFAS, endotoxin, viable bioburden, low-level organic speciation, and sub-10-nm particle precursors still depend heavily on sampling design, lab confirmation, validation, and data integrity.

The conclusion is not that online metrology has solved reclaimed water. The conclusion is more specific: in the sectors where reclaimed water is trying to enter high-spec processes, metrology is becoming the permission layer. Treatment makes the water plausible. Measurement makes it admissible.

In brief

  • Yes, in semiconductor fabs, online and rapid metrology is increasingly acting as the operational gatekeeper for whether reclaimed or reused water can proceed toward UPW makeup, cooling, or other fab uses.
  • Yes, in pharma/WFI, continuous TOC and conductivity are explicit quality gates, but microbiology and endotoxin still often require lab or validated rapid methods before release.
  • Partly, in battery manufacturing. High-purity and low-moisture requirements are real, but public evidence for named reclaimed-water-to-process acceptance gates is thin.
  • Partly, in data centers. Reclaimed water can be gated by cooling-water chemistry and microbiological control, but this is reliability water rather than product-contact high-spec manufacturing water.
  • No, not yet for all trace contaminants. PFAS, sub-10-nm particles, specific organics, endotoxin, and viable bioburden remain areas where offline sampling, validation, and false-result management are still central.
